Oganesson

Oganesson is a synthetic chemical element with the symbol 'Og' and atomic number 118. It is a superheavy element and belongs to Group 18 (or 8A) of the periodic table. Oganesson was first synthesized in 2002 by a team of Russian and American scientists led by Yuri Oganessian at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) in Dubna, Russia. The element was named in his honor. Oganesson is a synthetic element that is not found naturally on Earth.
